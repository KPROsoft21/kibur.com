My Timeline

  1. Introduction HTML

    NOV 2018

    I was first introduced to HTML during a student lecture at my school. A senior presented on web development and introduced us to HTML and the Adobe Dreamweaver editor. This marked the beginning of my programming journey.

  2. Introduction to Python

    OCT 2019

    In 2019, I acquired my first Raspberry Pi computer, a compact desktop with all the necessary tools for software and hardware projects. It included a printed Python tutorial that kickstarted my journey into becoming a proficient Python coder.

  3. CSS & Advanced HTML

    MAR 2020

    During the initial COVID school shutdown, we unexpectedly received an 8-month break instead of the anticipated 2 weeks. My friend and I decided to utilize this time to challenge ourselves by learning a new programming language. I chose to delve into HTML, and by the end of the extended break, I had become an HTML expert.

  4. JS, Flask & Django

    JUL 2020

    In July, I had an important realization that coding terminal apps might have limited utility. As a result, I redirected my focus towards scalable web applications. I discovered the potential of integrating Python, my main programming language, with frameworks to build comprehensive websites. Within just two months, I acquired this skill and successfully created a popular social media app called Table Chat, which enabled my classmates to connect during the COVID period. Additionally, I developed an advanced e-learning site for my school during the pandemic. Unfortunately, my efforts were met with disappointment as the assistant director rejected the project, citing a refusal to provide financial compensation to students. Despite this setback, I remained undeterred in my pursuit of programming and continued to explore new opportunities for growth.

  5. PyGame & Pyglet

    NOV 2020

    During our part-time return to school, my friend and I noticed that the kids' computer lab had been renovated. Unfortunately, all the retro games were deleted in the process. We saw this as an opportunity to contribute something meaningful, so we decided to create the games ourselves. I had just discovered the Python modules Pygame for 2D games and Pyglet for 3D ones. Utilizing my newfound skill of reverse-engineering, I analyzed existing code to learn how to create our own games. Over time, we developed a whole arcade of retro games for the kids' computer lab. Some of these games gained popularity, and we even sold them to other kids interested in using these games on their personal computers. Our initiative brought joy to many, and it was a rewarding experience that further fueled our passion for programming and game development.

  6. Advanced Python Programming

    DEC 2020

    After experiencing success with Table Chat and selling the arcade games, I felt inspired to leverage my programming skills to make a positive impact on society and also earn some money. I noticed various data management issues and decided to work on solving them. My initial target was to digitize the card catalog system for my school. However, despite my efforts, the school rejected my proposal for a minimal payment. Undeterred, I established my first money-making startup called KPRO soft. I honed my coding skills and database management, and I also taught my friends these valuable skills. Through KPRO soft, I sold custom code to numerous small businesses, including supermarkets and inventory companies owned by my friends' parents. Though most of the business was done through contacts, we were able to empower these businesses with reliable and affordable software solutions. It was a fulfilling journey as we contributed to the growth of various enterprises while making a significant impact in the process.

  7. UNITY programming

    APR 2021

    After exploring everything I could imagine with Pygame, I decided it was time for a change. Seeking advice on the best programming languages for game creation, I considered Unity or C as the top choices. Due to the high cost of Unreal Engine, I opted for Unity, a language that I had never truly mastered. Using my reverse engineering techniques, I recreated existing games and modified their dynamics. By piecing together segments of code, I managed to create some highly advanced games. However, Unity's complexity proved challenging, so I sought help from fellow developers I had connected with on GitHub. These individuals had prestigious degrees from top universities and high-paying jobs. They graciously completed and corrected my code, and in return, I offered my unique perspective as a child, helping them with their daily tasks at work. This collaborative exchange proved to be mutually beneficial and a valuable learning experience for me.

  8. Online Impact

    JUN 2021

    During the time of COVID, I found it difficult to focus on traditional learning, so I took advantage of off-days to establish an online presence and build a reputation. I began with small code problem-solving quizzes and gradually progressed to more significant challenges, like participating in PyWeek, the world's largest Python programming competition. Though I missed the top spot, I expected it as I chose to compete anonymously. However, since then, I have consistently performed well and won numerous coding competitions. In addition to competitions, I contributed to a global metaverse project, but I eventually withdrew due to policy conflicts. Nonetheless, I continued to achieve success, winning several hackathons and even hosting my own. The challenging circumstances of COVID didn't hinder my passion for programming and participating in coding events, leading me to expand my skills, make connections, and leave a positive impact in the programming community.

  9. Hacking

    NOV 2021

    During 10th grade, I embarked on a daring dare to hack a friend's account. Surprisingly, I succeeded, and this revelation about accessing people's data despite regulations fascinated me. This event marked the beginning of my journey into the world of Cyber-Security. Drawing from my experience, I also provided consultation to a local company on their IT security issues. This allowed me to apply my knowledge to practical situations and contribute positively by helping them enhance their security measures. My foray into Cyber-Security taught me the significance of ethical hacking and the critical role it plays in safeguarding digital assets and personal information. As I continued to learn and grow in this field, I aimed to use my skills to protect others and make a meaningful impact on the security of online spaces.

  10. Crypto Mining

    JAN 2022

    After Christmas, I came across a YouTube video about crypto mining, which piqued my interest due to my familiarity with blockchain technology. Using my Raspberry Pi, I tried mining Monero coins and earned around 0.02$ worth in a week. However, the mining server caused a major power outage in our area, taking two weeks to repair. This incident only fueled my determination to learn more about blockchain technology. I delved into extensive research, sought advice from various people, and read numerous books until I gained a solid understanding of building a cryptocurrency from scratch. Over the next few months, I developed my own cryptocurrency node and even had the opportunity to showcase it during one of my lectures at a local university.

  11. C++

    SEP 2022

    After dedicating four years to Python programming, I began considering a transition to a more powerful language. My choice was C++, a language that offered advanced capabilities while still maintaining some similarities to Python's mechanics. With C++, I entered a whole new coding universe, discovering a vast range of possibilities and opportunities for growth.

  12. Breakthrough

    FEB 2023

    In the midst of the demanding workload in upper high school, my coding projects took a backseat, and I hadn't worked on a real project since Junior Year. However, one weekend in early 2023, watching "The Social Network" sparked my inspiration. I was impressed by the effectiveness of Mark's projects at Harvard, motivating me to create something widely popular. That very night, I started working on a website that rates faces. While it might not sound like a viral idea, once word got out, the website's popularity soared, reaching up to 200 views a day. Although the popularity gradually declined as I became less active in updating the faces, even today, some kids opt to spend time on my website rather than TikTok. This experience gave me a taste of creating something massively popular, and it made me realize the potential for significant financial gain if I had monetized it. Nonetheless, the satisfaction of creating something impactful and engaging was invaluable.

  13. Typescript

    MAR 2023

    Javascript and Typescript are closely related languages, and I had often heard about Typescript in the context of company system software. I had briefly looked into it before, but my interest waned. However, during my internship, I was required to learn Typescript as part of the Angular framework tutorial. Within just two weeks, I grasped the basics, which opened up new avenues for my coding journey.
